Ford Motor Company

Control Systems Research Engineer

2016-2018

As the future of automotive industry and human mobility is tied to autonomous and electric cars, many vehicle subsystems are now undergoing revolutionary changes to adapt to the future needs. Exemplifying this in brake systems, a new technology is emerging to the cars we drive tomorrow. A novel brake system that can achieve higher deceleration rate decreasing the stopping distance, be integrated to autonomous platforms, provide more desirable pedal feel, and guarantee robustness and safety. In this role, I performed real-time hardware-in-the-loop control of the next generation of brake systems to set and validate the design specifications. To accomplish this, I developed vehicle dynamics models and control systems to simulate any desired vehicle maneuvers and implemented a virtual driver robot by controlling a linear actuator interfacing with the vehicle hardware such as brake pedal at a test bench setup.

Programming: Python, MATLAB
Software: Simulink, dSPACE, CarSim, CANalyzer, Datalyser
